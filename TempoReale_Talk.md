# Talk @ MAGNETICA 2026 | Tempo Reale
## Saturday, March 21, 2026

I am honored to participate in the second edition of **MAGNETICA**, the initiative by **Tempo Reale** dedicated to the evolving landscape of electroacoustic sound creation. 

The 2026 edition focuses on three contemporary themes: vocal research, algorithmic music, and **audiovision**. My contribution is part of the public program held at the **Auditorium Centro Rogers** in Scandicci (Florence).

###  Talk: Audiovision and Gesture
**Time:** 14:30  
**Venue:** Auditorium Centro Rogers (Piazzale della Resistenza 2/c, Scandicci, FI)  
**Moderator:** Agnese Banti 

In this session, I will present my research on the integration of **Computer Vision** and **Live Electronics**. The talk focuses on the dialectic between the physical gesture and the digital autonomous environment, specifically analyzing the "Ambiente 1" cycle.

**Abstract:**
*Ambiente 1: Elaboratore 58, the interactive interpretation of a visual artwork through MediaPipe Hands Landmarker, YOLO, and Live electronics*

In recent years, Artificial Intelligence (AI) has deeply permeated the creation and management of artistic performances, allowing for the expansion of human expressive and physical capabilities.This article presents the implementation and use of a control system based on the YOLO (You Only Look Once) library in combination with a MediaPipe-based system for musical and audiovisual performance. The primary objective is to demonstrate the efficacy of the system as a real-time partner capable of recognizing, interpreting, and reacting to specific objects and signs presented by the performer, and to highlight the importance of the musical gesture. The article details the design of a hybrid performance system. The system involves training YOLO for the identification of concrete objects that trigger audio and/or video processing algorithms, while the MediaPipe-based system is employed for continuous control and dynamic modulation of sound and visual parameters. This allows the interpreter to create and represent complex sonic gestures through interaction with the performance environment. This approach was applied to the interactive work "Ambiente 1: Elaboratore 58," which won a special mention in the "Musicare l'arte di Gianmaria Potenza" competition. The work was conceived for maximum adaptability, capable of operating both as a structured performance and as an open installation. In both configurations, the systems actto facilitate a continuous dialogue between gesture, sound, and image.Keywords: live electronics, AI computer vision, gesture recognition.
